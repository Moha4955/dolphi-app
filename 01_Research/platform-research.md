## 1. DUBIZZLE (UAE / MENA)

### Overview
- Dominant horizontal classifieds platform in UAE and MENA
- ~15 million monthly users | ~USD 750M annual revenue (Dubizzle Group)
- Backed by Dubizzle Group (preparing UAE IPO on Dubai Financial Market)
- Sister brands: Bayut (real estate), Hatla2ee (autos), Property Monitor (data/valuation)

### Core Categories
| Vertical | Revenue Share |
|---|---|
| Real Estate | 61% of UAE revenue |
| Autos | ~87% market share in UAE auto classifieds |
| Jobs | CV upload + applications |
| General Classifieds | Electronics, furniture, fashion, baby items |

### Key Innovations
- **Sell with AI**: Upload ONE photo → AI auto-generates title, description, category, price. 100,000+ listings in Month 1.
- **TruEstimate**: AI-powered automated valuation model (AVM), ~94-95% accurate, uses Dubai Land Department data.
- **TruBroker**: Agent leaderboard/ranking system. 6,500+ badges awarded. Agents earn TruPoints for responsiveness and listing quality.
- **TruBroker Stories**: World-first 60-second vertical video clips linked to real estate listings.
- **TruCheck**: Authenticates property listings as real.
- **AI Safety Layer**: 70 deployed AI models (64 proprietary), 49M predictions/month — category classification, malicious QR code detection in chat, sentiment analysis.

### Trust & Verification
- Users scan Emirates ID (both sides) + facial recognition = **Blue Tick Badge**
- 500,000+ verified users as of May 2025
- Verified users get boosted ad placement + priority support

### Business Model
- Free base listings
- Paid featured/premium placement
- B2B subscriptions (agents, dealers)
- Data products (TruEstimate)
- Display advertising

## 2. TRADEME (New Zealand)

### Overview
- Founded 1999, acquired by Apax Partners for NZD 2.56B in 2019
- 5 million active members in a country of 5.3 million adults — ~100% adult penetration
- NZD 375M annual revenue
- June 2025: Acquired 50% of Stuff Digital — merging editorial content with classifieds

### Categories
- Marketplace (general goods)
- Motors (cars, motorcycles, boats, caravans)
- Property (buy, rent, flatmates)
- Jobs (all sectors)
- Services (tradespeople)

### Auction + Buy Now Mechanics
- Sellers set reserve price + optional start price
- Auto-bidding up to buyer's maximum
- Buy Now available until reserve is met — then auction continues
- Sellers can restrict to authenticated members only

### MAJOR March 2026 Changes (direct response to Facebook Marketplace)
- **Eliminated 7.9% seller success fee for casual sellers**
- **Introduced buyer service fee**: NZD 0.99 / 1.99 / 4.99 depending on item value
- **Removed bank transfer** (was involved in 90% of unresolved scams)
- Now supports **Ping** (proprietary) + **Afterpay**
- Buyer Protection up to **NZD 5,000** on Ping/Afterpay transactions

### Trust & Safety
- Permanent feedback system (buyers + sellers leave mutual reviews)
- Community Watch — every listing has a report badge (community moderation)
- Dedicated Trust & Safety team, 7 days/week since 2007
- Buyer Protection: Non-delivery, item not as described, faulty goods covered

## 3. FACEBOOK MARKETPLACE

### Strengths
- 1 billion+ monthly users globally
- Free listings
- Social graph trust (mutual friends, profile visibility)
- Passive discovery (items surface in feed)
- Meta AI in chat (suggested questions to ask)
- eBay + Poshmark listing integration

### Critical Weaknesses (= Dolphi Opportunities)
1. **No buyer protection for local pickup** — the dominant transaction mode
2. **Scam epidemic** — 1 in 3 ads may be scams (TSB Bank research)
3. **Weak ratings** — individual comments not publicly visible
4. **No professional seller tools** — vehicles/property removed from Pages in 2023
5. **No listing quality standards** — unstructured free text, no condition grading
6. **No price benchmarking tools**
7. **Privacy exposure** — social identity deters privacy-conscious sellers
8. **Seller fee jumped from 5% → 10%** for delivery/checkout in 2025
9. **Chargeback risk** — buyers can dispute up to 3 months post-delivery

## 4. AUSTRALIAN CLASSIFIEDS MARKET

### Current Landscape (Fragmented)
| Vertical | Dominant Player | Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| General/Horizontal | Gumtree | AUD 74.4M FY2025 (declining) |
| Automotive | Carsales | AUD 1.1B FY2025 |
| Real Estate | REA Group | AUD 1.45B FY2024 |
| Jobs | Seek | AUD 1.23B |
| General C2C | Facebook Marketplace | Free (but unsafe) |

### Market Gaps (The Dolphi Opportunity)
1. **The "TradeMe Gap"**: No single trusted platform for all-of-life classifieds with payments + buyer protection
2. **Trust Vacuum**: 45% of Australians concerned about safety; AUD 8.6M lost to buying/selling scams in 2025; AUD 122M in online fraud in 9 months of 2025
3. **No Identity-Verified Marketplace**: No AU platform does government ID + biometric verification
4. **Poor Listing Quality**: No AI-assisted creation, no condition grading, no price benchmarking
5. **Fragmented Experience**: Australians need 4+ different platforms for goods, cars, property, jobs
6. **Recommerce Untapped**: AUD 60B+ second-hand economy, 35% of Aussies participate, growing 10%+/yr

### Australia's Unique Characteristics
- Population: 28.3M — mostly concentrated in 5 coastal metros
- 95% smartphone adoption but 73% mobile transaction abandonment (poor mobile checkout UX)
- 60% trust AI product recommendations (PayPal/YouGov 2025)
- Strong sustainability/pre-loved culture
- Mature digital identity infrastructure (myGov, Driver Licence APIs)
- Gumtree Pay launched May 2025 with **no buyer protection** — enormous gap
